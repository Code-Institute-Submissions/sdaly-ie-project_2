- History of the Game -

First there was Rock, Paper, Scissors! A traditional hand game believed to have originated from China more than two millennia ago. Whose original name was called "shoushiling" and is referenced in historical texts dated back to the Han dynasty (circa 206 BCE â€“ 220 CE). Where this game is thought to have then spread next to Japan and become known as "jan-ken." Before it arrived in the late 19th to early 20th century in Europe and America through trade, immigration and cultural exchange. However, it is only in more recent years that a new variant has appeared, known as: "Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock", which is a modified version of the game invented by Sam Kass and Karen Bryla sometime in the late 1990's to early 2000's. Where the game adds two more moves to the traditional Rock, Paper, Scissors game, which reduces the chances of having a tie. As in essence, Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock adds more complexity to the traditional game by offering players a greater range of moves and potential strategies. Thus, like the classic Rock, Paper, Scissors game, it is based on the principle that each move defeats certain moves or can draw. Utimately though this new variant of the game only truly become popularized after it featured in the hit TV show, called: "The Big Bang Theory" in which the character Sheldon explains and champions the game.
